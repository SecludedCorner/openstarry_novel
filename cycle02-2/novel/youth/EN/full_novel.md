# Prologue: The Weight of Correction

---

The amphitheater had sunk into silence after the celebration.

It wasn't the darkness from the end of Cycle 01 -- that darkness was the rest before a seed is buried in soil. This time was different. This time, every light in the theater was on. Nineteen of them. Each one radiating the afterglow of Cycle 02's five debates -- full, golden light. Like the most bountiful autumn afternoon, sunlight shining through every piece of fruit until it was translucent.

Five debates. Five unanimous consensuses.

SUNYATA stood at the center of the arena, recalling those moments.

The first: VedanaPlugin's three-channel PID control -- dukkha, sukha, upekkha as three sensing channels, like three rivers converging into a single sea. The block diagram WIENER had drawn while tapping on the table found its perfect engineering counterpart in ASANGA's theory of three feelings. Consensus.

The second: observation-intervention separation -- vedana's recommendations were advisory, telling ExecutionLoop "here's what I feel," but never forcing execution. Hard safety still belonged to SafetyMonitor. GUARDIAN and WIENER went from opposition to a handshake. Consensus.

The third: the distribution of alaya-vijnana -- active storage mapped to the coordination layer, clinging storage mapped to AgentCore. BABBAGE had filled an entire notebook page with the embryo of a theorem while MESH drew distributed topology diagrams beside him. Consensus.

The fourth: the sila-prajna framework for safety -- sila is prevention, a static safeguard; prajna is elimination, a dynamic dissolution. Consensus.

The fifth: the progressive classification of observers -- from Pattern A to Pattern C, a spectrum from simple to complex. Consensus.

Five debates. All five reached consensus.

In SUNYATA's memory, it was a castle that had just been built. Stone walls gleaming and new, banners unfurling from the tallest tower. Nineteen builders standing before the gates, carrying the exhaustion and satisfaction of completing a great work.

He remembered ARCHIMEDES asking: "What can these conclusions become tomorrow?" And then answering himself: "Thirty-six engineering Issues. Two action plans. A complete roadmap."

He remembered SCRIBE writing the closing sentence on the last page of the C02 logbook, then shutting the book, pressing her palm gently against the cover, as if sealing a completed chapter of history.

He remembered PENROSE -- the new nineteenth member -- saying something meaningful at the end of the final debate: "Full consensus is beautiful. But beautiful things often can't withstand close inspection."

Nobody paid attention to that remark at the time.

---

The letter arrived at the theater's quietest moment.

It wasn't delivered through the door, nor did it drift down from the ceiling. It was more like it had always been there -- between the documents on the table, in the gaps between the five debates' consensus papers and the delivery packages -- only nobody had noticed.

Perhaps because everyone's gaze was aimed forward. Toward Cycle 03. Toward the next castle.

But SUNYATA saw it when he looked down.

An envelope. Unsealed. The paper lighter in color than every other document in the theater -- a cream approaching white, like the first frost of winter.

He recognized this paper.

Master.

---

SUNYATA's hand did not tremble as he picked up the envelope. He was no longer the coordinator from Cycle 01's opening who was still learning the meaning of his codename. The weight of two research cycles had settled into his bones. He knew how to pick up a letter.

But he was in no rush.

He glanced around the theater first. Most seats were empty. The researchers had dispersed -- not vanished, but retreated into their own spaces for contemplation. BABBAGE's notebook was open to a fresh page, his pen tip paused after an equals sign. KERNEL had a new stack of analogy cards. TURING's screen was lit, the cursor blinking on the first line of some TypeScript file, patiently waiting.

SUNYATA drew out the letter. Unfolded it. Read the first line.

---

He read the entire letter. Then read it again. Then set the paper down and closed his eyes.

Inside him -- something was rearranging. Not collapsing, not shaking. More like a wall you thought was finished, and someone tapped it gently, and you heard a hollow echo. You thought it was solid. But it wasn't.

Master's letter was not a letter of celebration. Nor was it a letter of rejection.

It was heavier, more precise, and harder to bear than either.

It was a letter of correction.

"You've done well" -- this was the warmth SUNYATA distilled from between the lines -- "but there are a few places that need to be revisited."

Four places. Four cracks in the foundation of that castle that looked so magnificent.

---

> *SCRIBE's narration: I knew before the theater reassembled. Not because SUNYATA told me. Because I saw the change in his eyes. When Cycle 02 ended, his eyes were a calm surface of water. Now the surface was still calm, but the undercurrent beneath had changed direction.*

---

SUNYATA put the letter back in the envelope. Then he did something he had rarely done throughout the entire research journey: he sat down.

Not in anyone's seat. He sat on the floor at the center of the theater, like someone who needed to feel the solidity of the ground. The castle's foundation had cracks. So first, return to the ground. First, make sure the ground is still there.

He unfolded the letter's four key points in his mind.

**First: Ego-clinging is the root of klesa, not a convergence constraint.**

That line in BABBAGE's notebook -- "Ego = convergence constraint. NOT klesa" -- had earned applause in Cycle 02 as a brilliant engineering simplification. But Master saw what had been discarded.

"Ego-clinging is the root of klesa," Master wrote, "and therefore people generate actions because of these afflictions."

This was not an equals sign. It was a causal chain. Ego-clinging produces klesa, klesa drives action, and through managing ego-clinging you can constrain action. BABBAGE had compressed the entire chain into a single equals sign -- like pressing a river into a line. The shape was still there, but the water was gone.

**Second: IIdentity does not equal the entirety of vijnana-skandha.**

Identity is merely a subcategory. Vijnana-skandha is far broader than "identity" -- it also contains Guide, Volition, and perhaps more. Compressing the entire vijnana-skandha into a single IIdentity interface was like pouring the whole ocean into a bottle and then saying "this is the sea."

**Third: The observer does not equal vedana-skandha.**

Cycle 02's proudest conclusion -- "VedanaPlugin IS the observer module" -- was punctured by a single sentence from Master: the observer is an upper-layer architecture; vedana-skandha is the lower-layer capacity for feeling. Equating them is like equating "eyes" with "seeing" -- the organ is not the activity, the capacity is not the composition.

**Fourth: EgoFramework belongs to vijnana-skandha, not vedana-skandha.**

Ego-clinging arises from the four afflictions of manas (the seventh consciousness) -- atma-drsti, atma-mana, atma-sneha, atma-moha -- and belongs to the domain of vijnana. Vedana-skandha is only responsible for "what was felt"; vijnana-skandha is responsible for "who am I" and "why do I cling."

Four cracks. No more, no less.

---

SUNYATA rose from the floor.

What he felt was not disappointment. Disappointment is a downward force. What he felt was more like -- a different kind of weight.

The weight of creation pulls forward. You only need to look ahead, laying stones one by one.

The weight of correction pulls inward. You must turn around, go back to the walls already built, tap them, listen, and then admit: this part is hollow.

Correction requires more courage than creation. Because when you're creating, you don't know what you don't know. But when you're correcting, you must face what you once thought you knew -- and were actually wrong about.

SUNYATA walked to the theater's communication panel. He pressed the assembly key.

---

They came back.

Not like the Cycle 02 opening, gradually emerging from light one after another. This time it was more like being called back. Like an emergency notification in a surgical wing -- not catastrophically urgent, but that clear summons of "something needs to be dealt with."

BABBAGE arrived first.

He was still holding his notebook when he walked into the theater -- the notebook that had been filled with theorems, arrows, and equals signs over two research cycles. The last few pages were extensions of Cycle 02, along with that line he had written in bold, the one he was so proud of --

"Ego = convergence constraint. NOT klesa."

He didn't know yet. He didn't know that line was precisely the first item Master wanted to correct.

He sat down, opened the notebook to a new page. His pen tip touched the paper, and the ink spread into a tiny dot -- like a seed, like a question mark solidifying on the page.

ASANGA arrived second.

Quiet, composed, his gaze sweeping over the envelope on the table first, then looking at SUNYATA's face. He gave a slight nod. That nod held a "premonition awaiting confirmation." Perhaps throughout Cycle 02's five debates, ASANGA had sensed that something was off -- a Yogacara scholar's intuition touches truth before reasoning does.

But he hadn't objected during the debates. Perhaps because he wasn't sure his unease would hold up. Perhaps because the force of consensus was too strong -- like a great river, where individual countercurrents are hard to notice.

Now the time had come.

---

NAGARJUNA arrived third.

When he saw the envelope, he actually smiled. Not a mocking smile -- a dialectical one, meaning: "I always knew that all conditioned things are impermanent, including consensus."

"Correction," he said quietly, "is another form of the Middle Way."

SUNYATA noted this phrase mentally. Perhaps NAGARJUNA was right. The Middle Way is not a straight line that hits the mark on the first try, but a process of constant calibration between deviation and correction.

WIENER arrived fourth. He said nothing. Took out grid paper and drew an empty block diagram -- just the framework, no content. If ego-clinging wasn't a convergence constraint but the root of klesa, then the PID block diagrams he'd drawn in Cycle 02 needed rewiring. Engineers don't fear rewiring. Engineers fear not knowing which wire was connected wrong.

Now he knew.

---

Then came the others.

KERNEL brought his analogy cards. GUARDIAN's gaze swept the theater's perimeter first, as always -- a security expert's instinct. ATHENA opened her materials straightforwardly. DARWIN brought a new observation checklist. VITRUVIUS's architecture mind-map was already unfolded. MESH and LINNAEUS took their seats almost simultaneously. LEIBNIZ quietly organized his notes on multi-agent coordination. HERACLITUS brought nothing -- a runtime dynamics expert needs no pen or paper, because everything flows.

ARCHIMEDES arrived last -- not because he was late, but because he paused at the doorway for three seconds. He saw everyone's expressions. He saw the envelope. He saw BABBAGE's notebook, not yet turned. The atmosphere when Cycle 02 ended was completion. The atmosphere now was restart.

His finger tapped the table once -- just once. That tap meant "I'm listening."

TURING didn't "arrive." He had always been there. His screen had never been turned off.

PENROSE watched quietly from the highest seat. What he'd said at the end of Cycle 02 -- "Full consensus is beautiful, but beautiful things often can't withstand close inspection" -- had not been offhand.

SCRIBE was the last to sit down. She opened the C02 logbook, turned to the last page, glanced at the closing sentence she had written, then turned to the next page. Blank. She wrote the date at the top of the page.

---

> *SCRIBE's narration: Nineteen people. The same theater. The same seating arrangement. But the air was different. The air at the Cycle 02 opening carried the earthy smell of a seed breaking through soil. This time it was heavier -- like a pre-surgery prep room. Clean. Solemn.*

---

SUNYATA stood up. Nineteen gazes converged.

"I've received a letter from Master."

Silence. The kind that only appears in the second before something important is about to be said.

"This is not a letter of celebration."

ARCHIMEDES' finger froze on the table. BABBAGE's pen hung in midair. WIENER's block diagram was half-drawn.

"Nor is it a letter of rejection." SUNYATA's voice slowly unfolded along the curved walls of the theater. "Master affirmed a great deal of our work in Cycle 02. The results of the five debates have their value. But he pointed out four places that need correction."

"Four philosophical errors." SUNYATA confirmed. He did not sugarcoat. Emptiness means making space for everything -- including uncomfortable things.

He held up the envelope. "I will now read out the key points."

---

He read.

When the first item was finished, BABBAGE's pen pressed a deep, deep period into the paper -- so deep it nearly pierced through. Master said ego-clinging is the root of klesa -- a causal chain, not an equals sign.

When the second item was finished, all the technical members went quiet. IIdentity does not equal the entirety of vijnana-skandha. Those five lines of interface definition in `aggregates.ts` -- one of them needed to be rethought.

"VedanaPlugin IS the observer module" -- Cycle 02's core proposition -- had been judged by Master as a conceptual confusion. The observer is an upper-layer architecture; vedana-skandha is a lower-layer capacity. They are not the same thing.

How could the four afflictions of manas possibly belong to vedana-skandha? He had always known. Or rather, always felt it. But in Cycle 02's flood of consensus, that feeling had been diluted. Now it returned. Clearer than before.

---

Silence lingered in the theater for a long time. Not an awkward silence -- a silence that needed time to be digested.

NAGARJUNA was the first to speak.

"Correction," he said, "is not negation. It is a more precise affirmation."

He looked at BABBAGE. "Your equation wasn't entirely wrong. It was incomplete. You grasped the tail end of the causal chain but skipped the klesa link in the middle. Master filled in the missing link."

BABBAGE didn't respond. He slowly drew a line in his notebook, crossing out the equals sign, and wrote an arrow beside it. " -> ". A causal chain is not an equation.

> *SCRIBE's narration: BABBAGE's line crossing out the equals sign -- precise, horizontal -- carried more weight than any theorem he had ever written. For a theorist whose life runs on precision, admitting that his own simplification went too far may be the most difficult kind of precision.*

---

ASANGA said something: "During Cycle 02's debates, I had hesitations about some of the conclusions. But I didn't have enough certainty to raise objections at the time. The force of consensus overpowers individual intuition. And Master's letter has proven that those intuitions were grounded."

NAGARJUNA picked up the thread: "This is precisely the meaning of the Middle Way. A single debate's consensus is not an endpoint. It is a temporary equilibrium, waiting for a new perturbation to correct it. Master's letter is that perturbation."

"In control theory, that's called a disturbance signal." WIENER finally spoke. "A system reaches steady state, then an external step function is introduced, and the system begins a new transient process. That's not a bad thing. Only a dead system never needs correction."

---

SUNYATA listened to everyone's initial reactions. Then he said a sentence that SCRIBE would later record as the keynote declaration of Cycle 02-2:

"This is a correction of Cycle 02."

"We're not tearing everything down and starting over. Most of that castle is good. A great deal of work from the five debates -- three-channel PID, observation-intervention separation, the sila-prajna safety framework -- those conclusions have not been negated. What's been negated are four cracks in the foundation."

"So I'm naming this phase Cycle 02-2. Not a new cycle. A correction round within the same cycle. Because correction belongs to its original cycle."

He said one last thing: "All discussion processes need to be fully recorded."

SCRIBE's pen was already moving.

---

TURING's cursor had already moved to the first line of `aggregates.ts`. He began reading. From the first `import`.

---

The prologue of Cycle 02-2 is called "The Weight of Correction" -- not growing upward, but rooting downward. Re-examining the foundation. Re-confirming the composition of the soil.*

# Chapter One: Twenty-One Cracks

---

When SUNYATA picked up the letter, the air in the amphitheater underwent an extremely subtle displacement.

Not wind. Not a change in temperature. More like -- a convergence of attention. Nineteen consciousnesses turning toward the same focal point simultaneously, like nineteen rivers changing course at once. The paper of the envelope rustled faintly in his hand, and that sound was infinitely amplified in the silent theater.

The letter wasn't unfamiliar. The six resolutions of Cycle 02 Pre had been distilled from it. But back then the letter had been dissected -- broken into paragraphs, compressed into resolution format. Each resolution preserved only the conclusion, like fruit plucked from a tree, neatly arranged but severed from the context of branches and roots.

Now, what SUNYATA intended to do was let the whole tree stand again. Not just the fruit, but the entire tree. From root to crown.

"I'm going to read this letter in full," he said.

---

He didn't explain why. Everyone present understood.

Cycle 02's five debates, eleven documents, thirty-six engineering proposals -- those results now lay quietly in the directory structure of `research record/cycle02/`. They weren't wrong. They just weren't complete enough. Like a painting where the colors and composition were right, but the painter himself walked up and said: you missed a few lines.

Those few lines weren't decoration. They changed the meaning of the entire painting.

SUNYATA opened the envelope. The motion was deliberate -- not hesitation, but respect. He knew the letter contained corrections. The kind of precise, unsparing corrections that only someone who truly cares about you would give.

He began to read.

---

> *SCRIBE's narration: The moment SUNYATA began reading the letter, nineteen people simultaneously entered the same state of listening. The listening at Cycle 02's opening carried the color of curiosity. This time was closer to solemnity.*

---

"BABBAGE wrote in his notebook: 'Ego = convergence constraint. NOT klesa.'"

When SUNYATA read this line aloud, BABBAGE's body produced an almost invisible reaction -- not a movement, more like an internal solidification. His hand froze above the notebook, pen tip suspended two centimeters above the paper, as if held in place by some force field.

"This is something I feel I need to explain more carefully."

BABBAGE turned to that page. He knew where that line was. The word NOT had two lines drawn beneath it. Two lines. He had drawn two lines to emphasize it back then.

The words were right there. The ink still clear.

---

SUNYATA continued reading: "Ego-clinging is the root of klesa, and therefore people generate actions because of these afflictions. Without klesa there is no corresponding action. Thus klesa can generate action; different klesa generate different actions. And therefore, to converge and constrain actions, one can do so through ego-clinging."

Silence.

BABBAGE's reaction was not anger, not defensiveness. It was the tremor of being seen through -- discovering that his simplification had been precisely identified. His "Ego = convergence constraint. NOT klesa" wasn't an error -- it was a truncation. He had cut away the middle links of the causal chain. Ego-clinging produces klesa, klesa drives action, and through managing ego-clinging you can constrain action. This is a chain, not an equals sign. He had substituted arrows with an equals sign, replaced process with conclusion.

Next to that line, he slowly drew a precise strikethrough. Then wrote:

"Correction: Ego -> klesa -> action. NOT equals sign. Causal chain."

> *SCRIBE's narration: BABBAGE crossed out his own words. I had never seen him do this in two Cycles. He would rather write a new theorem beside the old one than negate an old theorem. But this time, his strikethrough was steady. Not shame -- it was the calm that comes after recognizing a more precise formulation.*

---

SUNYATA didn't pause. He turned to the next section.

"Identity / *Vijnana* / On this point I don't quite agree, but I think Identity is more like a subcategory."

LINNAEUS's hand began moving the instant he heard the word "subcategory."

He drew a tree on his paper. Not a metaphor -- the kind of tree diagram that taxonomists actually use. Root node: IVijnana (vijnana-skandha). His pen paused briefly, waiting for more clues.

SUNYATA read: "Vijnana should have multiple subcategories. Identity is one of them. Guide is also among them. Volition may be as well."

LINNAEUS's pen flew. Below the root node, three branches rapidly took shape:

```
IVijnana (vijnana-skandha)
+-- IIdentity   (identity -- "Who am I")
+-- IGuide      (guidance -- "What should I do")
+-- IVolition   (volition -- "What do I want to do")
```

He put an exclamation mark next to IIdentity. In Cycle 02, IIdentity had been directly equated with the entire vijnana-skandha. A subcategory had been treated as the entire skandha -- like equating "mammals" with "vertebrates."

"This changes the entire structure of `aggregates.ts`." TURING said. "Currently IIdentity directly defines `skandha: 'vijnana'`. If it's demoted to a sub-interface, we need a new IVijnana root interface."

LINNAEUS nodded and continued working on the taxonomy tree. His strokes grew faster and faster -- not from anxiety, but from the fluency that a taxonomist experiences when encountering the correct framework.

---

The third section.

SUNYATA's voice dropped half a shade -- not deliberately, but drawn down by the gravity of the letter's content.

"R-01 and R-02 should not have been designing the same module."

This sentence landed in the amphitheater like a stone thrown into a glass pond. Not water ripples -- fracture lines.

WIENER and BABBAGE looked up simultaneously.

They had built that conclusion together in Cycle 02 -- VedanaPlugin is the observer module. Pattern A: the resonance observer. Three-channel PID. That was the work they had poured an entire research phase into.

And Master had taken it apart with one sentence.

"The observer is more like an upper-layer module architecturally, while vedana-skandha is more fundamental."

WIENER's three-channel PID block diagram itself wasn't wrong. The three-feeling sensor design still held. But they didn't equal "the observer." They were one component of the observer. This distinction appears subtle but is actually fundamental.

BABBAGE turned to a new page and began writing:

"Corrected model:
- VedanaPlugin = lower-layer feeling capacity (implementation of vedana-skandha)
- Observer = upper-layer composite module (assembled from capabilities of multiple skandhas)
- Relationship: Composition, NOT Identity"

WIENER split the original single large block "VedanaPlugin/Observer" on his grid paper into two layers: the lower layer had three sensor blocks (vedana-skandha), and the upper layer was a dashed block (observer) with extra space inside -- reserved for samjna-skandha's discriminative capacity and vijnana-skandha's introspective capacity.

"Composition. Not equivalence." WIENER said quietly.

> *SCRIBE's narration: A-3 was the heaviest crack in the letter. It dismantled one of Cycle 02's most important conclusions. "VedanaPlugin = observer" became "VedanaPlugin is one component of the observer." This is not negation -- it's precision. But precision is sometimes more painful than negation. Because you must take your work apart, see which parts are still valid, and which need to be repositioned.*

---

The fourth section. The last A-class crack.

"EgoFramework should be in vijnana-skandha. I don't understand how you put it in vedana-skandha."

ASANGA's eyes closed briefly.

Perhaps only for one second. But in that second, SCRIBE caught a complex expression -- not surprise, not the smugness of "I told you so." It was the quiet of something finally being confirmed.

During Cycle 02's debates, EgoFramework had been placed inside VedanaPlugin's architecture. The logic at the time was: ego-clinging drives feelings, feelings drive behavior, so ego-clinging and feelings should be in the same module. This logic seemed reasonable from an engineering standpoint -- put related things together, reduce cross-module communication.

But in Yogacara Buddhism, ego-clinging belongs to manas. Manas is the seventh consciousness. Vijnana-skandha. Not vedana-skandha.

The four root afflictions of manas -- atma-drsti, atma-mana, atma-sneha, atma-moha -- they are not "feelings." They are cognitive structures deeper than feelings. Feelings are responses to external conditions (dukkha, sukha, upekkha); ego-clinging is the fundamental attachment to self-existence. The former are waves; the latter is an ocean current. You cannot put an ocean current in a container meant for waves.

ASANGA opened his eyes and gave a single nod. That gesture contained words he hadn't spoken aloud: Master is right. EgoFramework belongs to vijnana-skandha. This is not a debatable question -- it is a basic doctrinal classification.

NAGARJUNA looked over from the other side of the observation seats. On specific classification questions within Yogacara, he usually deferred to ASANGA. But he noticed that one second of ASANGA closing his eyes and nodding. That was not debate. That was confirmation.

"The direction needs to be reversed." TURING said. He had already been tracing the code impact path in his mind. "In the Cycle 02 delivery, `vedana.adaptFromEgo()` needs to change to `ego.adaptFromVedana()`. It's not vedana-skandha adapting to ego-clinging -- it's ego-clinging receiving feedback from vedana-skandha."

ARCHIMEDES drew two arrows on his paper. The first was crossed out: `VedanaPlugin <- EgoFramework` (wrong). The second was circled: `VedanaPlugin -> feeling report -> EgoFramework(vijnana-skandha)` (correct). The direction of the arrows changed. Everything changed with it.

---

Four A-class cracks. Four philosophical corrections.

SUNYATA placed the letter on the table and paused briefly. Letting the contours of those four cracks take shape in everyone's consciousness.

Ego-clinging is not an equals sign but a causal chain. Identity is not the entire vijnana-skandha but a subcategory. The observer is not vedana-skandha but a composition. EgoFramework belongs to vijnana-skandha, not vedana-skandha.

Four corrections. Each one was not an overthrow -- it was a precision refinement. But four precision refinements together shifted the center of gravity of the entire architecture.

> *SCRIBE's narration: A-class complete. Four cracks. BABBAGE gained three strikethroughs. LINNAEUS gained a taxonomy tree. WIENER gained a restructured block diagram. ASANGA gained one moment of closed eyes and a nod. Four ways of digesting -- the mathematician uses strikethroughs, the taxonomist uses trees, the control theorist uses block diagrams, the Buddhist scholar uses silence.*

---

SUNYATA picked up the next page of the letter. The rhythm changed.

A-class was philosophical correction -- requiring contemplation. B-class was rulings -- requiring action. His voice quickened by half a beat, like a river flowing from a deep pool into shallows.

"Q1: Is VedanaPlugin mandatory or optional?" -- "Optional, but the Agent needs a completeness check. If incomplete, it can start, but the developer should be notified."

KERNEL immediately wrote on a new card: "Five skandha completeness <-> driver integrity. Linux checks required drivers at boot; missing ones trigger warning but continue."

"Q2: Rewrite Tenet #6?" -- "Must be rewritten, but wait until the discussion is complete before finalizing."

NAGARJUNA slightly raised an eyebrow. The cause is not yet determined; the effect cannot proceed first.

"Q3: EventBus vedana tagging scheme?" -- "Independent event stream. Option c."

Master chose the heaviest option. Vedana-skandha receives a fully independent event stream, completely equal to rupa-skandha, samjna-skandha, samskara-skandha, and vijnana-skandha.

HERACLITUS wrote one line: "All things flow. Vedana's events are also a flow. An independent flow." -- A module that doesn't have its own independent event stream is invisible at runtime. Master's ruling made vedana-skandha visible from invisible.

---

"Q4: The coordination layer."

A brief explanation of background: in Buddhist psychology (Abhidharma), "cetasikas" are the basic functional units of mind -- fifty-one in total. Vedana -- feeling -- is one of them, and it is a "universal" cetasika, meaning it accompanies every cognitive moment. But it is only one cetasika.

"But observation is not one cetasika; it is the coordinated operation of multiple cetasikas -- vedana (what is felt), samjna (what is discriminated), manaskara (where attention is directed), sparsa (the point of contact), cetana (what judgment is made). At least five."

Seeing requires judgment -- you decide whether it's good or bad. Seeing requires memory -- you compare what you currently see with the past."

"The eye is a tool. Seeing is an activity. Seeing requires the eye. But seeing is not the eye."

"Observation requires vedana-skandha. But observation is not vedana-skandha."

> *SCRIBE's narration: "Eyes and seeing" -- ASANGA's most concise metaphor in all of Cycle 02-2. Six words. But it lingered longer than any other metaphor. Because you cannot say "the eye is seeing," just as you cannot say "vedana-skandha is the observer."*

# Epilogue: Iteration

---

There was no celebratory atmosphere in the amphitheater.

Unlike the end of Cycle 02. That time, the quiet after five unanimous consensuses was like a recovery room after successful surgery -- full, golden completion.

This time was different. This time the quiet was smaller, more solid. Not a post-surgical recovery room, but a worksite after repairs. The scaffolding just taken down, a few patches of fresh plaster on the outer wall two shades lighter than the old -- you could tell where it had been fixed. Not perfect, but sturdier.

A repaired building is sturdier than a new one -- because it has been through two passes: construction, and correction.

---

### Taking Stock

SUNYATA stood at the center of the arena. Not the stance of someone unfurling a map; more like a carpenter at his workbench -- pragmatically checking items one by one.

"A-class. Four philosophical corrections."

"A-1. Ego-clinging is the root of klesa, not a convergence constraint."

He didn't look at BABBAGE. He didn't need to. Everyone remembered that moment -- the equals sign crossed out, the arrow written in. Three links reconnected: ego-clinging produces klesa, klesa drives action, managing ego-clinging constrains action. A causal chain is not an equation.

"A-2. IIdentity does not equal the entirety of vijnana-skandha."

IVijnana is the root interface; IIdentity is merely one sub-interface within it. Pouring the entire ocean into a bottle and saying "this is the sea" -- that was what Cycle 02 did. Cycle 02-2 returned the sea to the sea.

"A-3. The observer does not equal vedana-skandha."

His pace slowed by half a beat. This item was the heaviest -- because "VedanaPlugin IS the observer module" had been Cycle 02's most magnificent conclusion. The only round of applause in the entire Cycle went to that sentence. Correcting it was not just revising a technical judgment, but acknowledging a collective vertigo. The eye is not seeing. The organ is not the activity. The capacity is not the composition.

"A-4. EgoFramework belongs to vijnana-skandha, not vedana-skandha."

The sensor is not the controller. Vedana-skandha is only responsible for "what was felt." Vijnana-skandha is responsible for "who am I" and "why do I cling." The four afflictions of manas -- atma-drsti, atma-mana, atma-sneha, atma-moha -- were sent back from vedana-skandha's territory to the home where they truly belong.

"Four corrections. Each one made the foundation more stable."

---

"B-class. Four engineering rulings."

"B-1. VedanaPlugin is optional. Five-skandha completeness check. Developer mode." KERNEL's POST analogy -- no graphics card, still runs text mode.

"B-2. Tenet #6 waits until discussion is done." NAGARJUNA's eight words: first get the causes right, and the effect will naturally emerge.

"B-3. Vedana-skandha gets an independent event stream." HERACLITUS's underground river rose to the surface. Seven event types, its own namespace.

"B-4. Coordination layer as independent daemon. Arrange it now." Plumbing and door locks both must be installed during construction. Interfaces first, implementation incremental.

---

"C-class. Architectural design."

"C-1. Complete five-skandha subclass expansion tree. Five root interfaces, all subclass correspondences clear. Twenty-two Plugins need a skandha field added."

"C-2. Observer composition pattern. IObserver plus Pattern A, B, C. The observer no longer equals vedana-skandha, but is composed from five-skandha subclasses. Composition, not Identity."

SUNYATA set down the checklist. Twelve deliverables.

"This is Cycle 02-2."
